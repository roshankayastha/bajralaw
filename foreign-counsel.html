    Financial Law

    Banking & Finance Legal Services in Nepal

    Bajra Law & Associates has a strong track record advising banks, development finance institutions, microfinance institutions, and corporate borrowers on complex financial transactions in Nepal. Our banking and finance practice is deeply integrated with Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB) regulations and Securities Board of Nepal (SEBON) guidelines.

    We advise on the full range of debt finance transactions, from straightforward bilateral loans to complex multi-tranche project financings. Our team has particular expertise in hydropower and infrastructure project finance, which is central to Nepal's economic development agenda.

    We are also experienced in advising on the regulatory aspects of establishing banking and financial institutions, including 'B', 'C', and 'D' class financial institution licences, as well as the regulatory requirements for payment service operators under the Payment and Settlement Act.

    What We Offer

    Key Services

    Loan Documentation

    Drafting and negotiating facility agreements, security documentation, guarantees, and intercreditor arrangements for commercial and development finance transactions.

    Project Finance

    Legal advisory for hydropower, infrastructure, and industrial project financings, including security package structuring and lender-side representation.

    NRB Regulatory Compliance

    Advising financial institutions on NRB directives, capital adequacy compliance, Basel framework implementation, and periodic regulatory reporting obligations.

    Loan Syndications

    Structuring and executing syndicated loan transactions, including preparation of information memoranda, lead arranger agreements, and participation arrangements.

    Securities & Capital Markets

    IPO advisory, bond issuance, SEBON registration, and compliance for public companies listing on the Nepal Stock Exchange (NEPSE).

    Financial Institution Licensing

    Licensing applications for commercial banks, development banks, finance companies, and microfinance institutions before Nepal Rastra Bank.
